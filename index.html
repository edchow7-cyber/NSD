The problem

AI Is Unusable on the Data That Matters Most.

Your most valuable data — customers, transactions, contracts, patient records — is also your most restricted. Today, that data sits on the sidelines while AI transforms every other part of the business.

The hesitation is grounded in three real risks compliance, legal, and security teams can't ignore.

Regulatory exposure

Sending sensitive data to public LLMs can breach GDPR, HIPAA, SOX, and a stack of internal policies. The fines, audits, and breach notifications outweigh any AI upside.

→ Audit failure

IP leakage

Your pricing models, customer behavior, and trade secrets flow into systems you don't control. Many providers retain prompts. Some use them to train future models — your data, training your competitors' AI.

→ Competitive risk

No way to govern

Once data leaves your perimeter, you can't audit who saw it, prove it was deleted, or pull it back. Security and compliance teams have no choice but to block — and the most valuable AI use cases die in committee.

→ Compliance blocker
The solution

Nera Secure Data Analyst.

Three risks. Three answers. One product that lets you use commercial LLMs on your most sensitive data — without ever exposing it.

ENCRYPTION

No data leaves unencrypted

Encryption happens client-side, before a single byte moves. Public LLMs never see plaintext. Your existing GDPR, HIPAA, and SOX posture stays intact.

PRIVACY

The model can't read your data

Commercial LLMs operate directly on ciphertext. There's nothing for providers to log, retain, or train on — because there's nothing for them to see.

CONTROL

Security stays in command

Enterprise controls let your security and compliance teams monitor every prompt, gate user access, and audit complete usage. Your private key never leaves your environment — so AI runs under the same governance as any other critical system.
